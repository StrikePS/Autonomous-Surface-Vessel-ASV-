Fixed landmarks are placed on the environment. 
This provides us with fixed points on the env to have global positioning of the vessel.
The landmarks are responsible for vessel observation and reporting distance to vessel and bearing.
These measeurements localise the vessel in the given environment.

How to make landmarks? 
1. Using markers. The vessel hosts cameras that rotate or placed on all sides that capture the markers using openCV and measure range and bearing for localization. 

2. infrared sensors. The landmarks have a host of senors that return the range to the vessel. Bearing may be tricky to establish. 

3. Ultra wide band modules. Fixed anchors on the landmarks with multiple anetnnas. Bearing may be tricky to establish. 

4. Lidar Reflectors. Reflective poles or retroreflectors on the vessel. Use the Lidar scan peaks. Both range and bearing easily and directly.
   Cons: Water surface is also reflective and can mess up measurement.


Hardware Required:
1. 2D Lidar
2. Retro Reflecters
   or, Highly Reflective Tape
3. Landmark mounting, aluminium or steel poles with concrete base.
4. Calibration tools, tape or laser rangefinder.
